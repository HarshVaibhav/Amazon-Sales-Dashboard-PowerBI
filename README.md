# Amazon-Sales-Dashboard-PowerBI
Interactive Amazon Sales Dashboard built using Power BI, DAX, and Power Query for sales, customer, product, and operational analysis.

## Project Overview

This project analyzes Amazon sales data using Power BI.

The dashboard provides insights into:

- Total Revenue
- Total Orders
- Total Customers
- Average Order Value
- Return Rate
- Product Performance
- Revenue by Category
- Revenue by State
- Payment Method Analysis
- Order Status Analysis

## Tools Used

##Key Insights

• Generated total revenue of 91.83M from over 100K orders and 43K customers.

• Average Order Value (AOV) stood at 918.26, indicating strong customer spending per transaction.

• Credit Card emerged as the highest revenue-generating payment method, followed by Debit Card and UPI.

• A small number of products contributed disproportionately to total revenue, highlighting the importance of top-performing products.

• Revenue was distributed across multiple product categories, with Electronics and Home & Kitchen contributing significantly to overall sales.

• Order fulfillment performance remained strong, with the majority of orders successfully delivered and a low return rate of 2.83%.

• Sales trends showed seasonal fluctuations throughout the year, suggesting opportunities for targeted promotional campaigns during peak periods.


##Areas for Improvement

• Investigate products with higher return rates and identify root causes such as quality issues, pricing concerns, or delivery delays.

• Increase adoption of high-value payment channels through targeted discounts and cashback offers.

• Optimize inventory planning for top-performing products to prevent stock shortages during peak demand periods.

• Focus marketing efforts on underperforming categories to improve revenue diversification.

• Analyze state-level performance and replicate successful sales strategies from high-performing regions.

• Implement customer retention initiatives to increase repeat purchases and improve customer lifetime value.

• Track profit margins alongside revenue to identify products generating high sales but low profitability.
